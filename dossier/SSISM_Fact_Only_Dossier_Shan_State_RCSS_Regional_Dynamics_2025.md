## **Shan State, RCSS & Regional Dynamics (2025)**

# **PART I — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**



This dossier presents a fact-only analysis of the political, military, and regional dynamics involving Shan State and the Restoration Council of Shan State (RCSS) during 2023–2025.  

All content is:



- Verified by public statements  

- Cross-checked with OSINT  

- Grounded in conflict monitoring data  

- Stripped of unverified claims and speculation  



The objective is to establish **a neutral, evidence-based, and credible** baseline for understanding Shan State’s evolving political environment.



---



# **PART II — VERIFIED FACTS: SHAN STATE SECURITY ENVIRONMENT**



## **2.1. Key Armed Actors**

The following actors are confirmed active in Shan State:



- **RCSS/SSA-S** – Primarily southern Shan  

- **SSPP/SSA-N** – Northern Shan  

- **TNLA** – Palaung region & northern Shan  

- **UWSA** – Wa autonomous areas  

- **SAC** – Myanmar military throughout the state  



Data sourced from: conflict monitors, NGO reports, and multiple independent OSINT streams.



---



# **PART III — RCSS CURRENT POSTURE (2023–2025)**



## **3.1. RCSS–SAC Military Relationship (Verified)**



- RCSS **is not conducting major offensive operations** against SAC.  

- RCSS **did not join** Operation 1027 or the anti-SAC alliance.  

- RCSS **did not sign any new cooperation agreement** with SAC after the 2021 coup.  

- RCSS maintains a **ceasefire posture** and remains a signatory of the 2015 Nationwide Ceasefire Agreement.



**Conclusion:**  

RCSS is **not pro-SAC**, but is **not fighting SAC**.  

The posture is **neutral / non-aligned**.



---



# **PART IV — RCSS RELATIONS WITH OTHER SHAN GROUPS**



### **4.1. RCSS vs SSPP/TNLA**



- Tensions peaked between 2018–2021 due to territorial disputes.  

- RCSS withdrew from northern Shan after pressure from SSPP/TNLA and local communities.  

- Verified: **Clashes have sharply decreased since 2022** due to geographic separation and shifts in regional military dynamics.



---



# **PART V — REGIONAL ACTORS & BORDER DYNAMICS**



### **5.1. Thailand**

- RCSS HQ located on the Thai border (Loi Tai Leng).  

- Thailand exerts strict controls and has **not encouraged** RCSS participation in conflict.



### **5.2. China**

- Primary influence over UWSA, SSPP, and MNDAA.  

- **No confirmed evidence** of direct Chinese support or pressure directed at RCSS.



### **5.3. Border Network Dynamics**

- Long history of cross-border trade and migration.  

- No evidence linking RCSS to cartel operations.

# **PART VI — INFORMATION CHECK: RCSS & SHAN PRINCE LINK**

# **PART VII — WHY RCSS CANNOT BE CLASSIFIED AS PRO-MILITARY (SAC)**



Verified facts:



- RCSS did **not** support SAC offensives  

- RCSS criticized the military’s political failures  

- RCSS territory was encroached upon by SAC-allied operations in 2021–2023  

- RCSS lost ground due to its neutral posture  



**RCSS = Neutral, regionally focused, non-participatory.**
