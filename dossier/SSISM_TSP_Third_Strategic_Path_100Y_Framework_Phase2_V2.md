## 1. Problem Definition: The Structural Failure of Binary Choices

### 1.1 The Authoritarian Security Model (Evidence Anchored)

Empirical indicators demonstrate consistent outcomes under military governance in Myanmar:

‚Ä¢ **Conflict persistence:** UCDP conflict datasets show uninterrupted internal armed conflict since independence, with intensity increasing after prolonged military rule.  
‚Ä¢ **Economic degradation:** World Bank and IMF data (2010‚Äì2020) indicate chronic underperformance relative to ASEAN peers despite resource endowments.  
‚Ä¢ **Sanctions dependency:** UN and EU sanctions regimes repeatedly correlate with capital flight and informalisation of the economy.  
‚Ä¢ **Illicit economy reliance:** UNODC reports document narcotics, jade, timber, and scam-related revenues sustaining armed actors.

**Conclusion:** Security-first authoritarianism empirically reduces state capacity over time.

---

### 1.2 The Fragile Electoral Model (Evidence Anchored)

Comparative transitions reveal limitations of elections without institutional depth:

‚Ä¢ **Reversibility:** Myanmar (2021), Thailand (multiple cycles), and Pakistan demonstrate electoral rollback when civilian control of security is absent.  
‚Ä¢ **Centralisation bias:** Constitutional centralism persists despite electoral change (e.g., Myanmar 2008 Constitution).  
‚Ä¢ **Ethnic exclusion:** Ethnic armed organisations remain outside formal governance despite electoral participation.

**Conclusion:** Elections without structural reform do not secure democracy.
