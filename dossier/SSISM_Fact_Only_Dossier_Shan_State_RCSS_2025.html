1. Situation Overview

Shan State in 2025 remains one of the most strategically important, politically complex, and militarily fragmented theatres in Myanmar’s conflict landscape. Unlike other regions dominated by two-sided conflict (PDF vs. SAC), Shan State continues to host multi-actor competition among:

  • RCSS/SSA (Restoration Council of Shan State)
  • TNLA (Ta’ang National Liberation Army)
  • MNDAA (Myanmar National Democratic Alliance Army)
  • UWSA (United Wa State Army)
  • SAC-aligned militias / Border Guard Forces
  • Local Pa-O, Shan, and other ethnicity-based militias

2. RCSS: Operational Position (2025)

The RCSS remains under sustained pressure from the TNLA–MNDAA–UWSA alliance. Their territorial losses since 2021 have drastically limited maneuvering capability. Key factual trends:

  • Loss of key strongholds to TNLA and MNDAA expansions.
  • Increased reliance on cross-border safe zones.
  • Strained relations with local Shan communities in northern zones.
  • Attempts to position themselves politically for the post-coup era.
  • Reduced battlefield initiative compared to 2015–2019 peak era.

3. TNLA & Northern Alliance Expansion

The TNLA’s 2023–2025 offensives reshaped northern Shan State. Supported operationally and logistically by MNDAA and UWSA, TNLA has:

  • Consolidated historical Ta’ang territories.
  • Secured several major trade routes.
  • Expanded administrative structures in controlled zones.
  • Improved revenue from controlled checkpoints and taxation.

4. China’s Strategic Layer

China maintains a “multi-vector neutrality” approach:

  • Engagement with UWSA as a trusted proxy force.
  • Working channels with SAC for border security.
  • Tactical tolerance of MNDAA territorial expansion.
  • Strict interest in stabilizing cross-border economic corridors.

Chinese intelligence assessments consistently prioritize:

  1. Curbing transnational crime compounds.
  2. Maintaining uninterrupted border trade.
  3. Avoiding refugee spillover.
  4. Preventing U.S., Thai, or Indian influence from expanding into Shan State via the resistance movement.

5. SAC Position in Shan State (2025)

The junta maintains fragmented but still significant presence:

  • Urban administrative nodes (Taunggyi, Kengtung, Lashio).
  • Key military bases and airfields.
  • Partnerships with Pa-O and other pro-military militias.

However, SAC’s combat capability is heavily constrained due to:

  • Troop shortages.
  • Constant pressure from Northern Alliance offensives.
  • Declining morale and mass desertions.
  • Inability to reassert control in remote mountainous areas.

6. Regional Scenario Forecast (2025–2026)

Scenario A — Continued Multi-Actor Fragmentation

Most probable scenario. No single actor can dominate Shan State.

Scenario B — TNLA/MNDAA Consolidation

Likely in northern and northeastern zones.

Scenario C — RCSS Strategic Realignment

Possible political pivot toward FPNCC coordination or NUG-linked negotiation channels.

Scenario D — China-Imposed Stabilization

Triggered if:

  • Border trade is threatened.
  • Crime compounds re-emerge.
  • Refugee flows affect Yunnan.

7. Final Assessment

Shan State will remain a geopolitical “grey zone” through 2025 and 2026. No single armed group can control the state, and external actors—especially China—remain the decisive force shaping outcomes.
