### Executive Summary

The Naypyidaw government repeatedly accuses ASEAN of violating the ASEAN Charter by “interfering in Myanmar’s internal affairs.” This claim is a **selective and misleading interpretation** (cherry-picking) of the Charter.

The ASEAN Charter contains **14 principles** under Article 2. The regime focuses only on non-interference while ignoring commitments to democracy, human rights, rule of law, and regional peace and stability.

---

### Fact-Check: What the ASEAN Charter Actually Says

**Article 2 – Principles** of the ASEAN Charter lists **14 specific principles**.

Key relevant principles include:

- Respect for the independence, sovereignty, equality, territorial integrity and national identity of all ASEAN Member States.
- **Non-interference in the internal affairs** of ASEAN Member States.
- Adherence to the rule of law, good governance, the principles of democracy and constitutional government.
- Respect for fundamental freedoms, the promotion and protection of human rights, and the promotion of social justice.
- Shared commitment and collective responsibility in enhancing regional peace, security and prosperity.
- Upholding the United Nations Charter and international law.

**Important:** Sovereignty and non-interference are **not absolute**. They must be balanced with other principles, especially when a situation threatens regional stability.

---

### Why the Regime’s Accusation is Misleading

1. **The junta itself agreed to the Five-Point Consensus**  
   In April 2021, the military leadership formally agreed to the ASEAN Five-Point Consensus, which includes immediate cessation of violence, inclusive political dialogue, and humanitarian access.  
   The regime has failed to implement the agreement it signed.

2. **The crisis has major regional impact**  
   Millions of refugees, transnational crime, drug trafficking, and border instability directly affect ASEAN member states. This is no longer a purely internal matter.

3. **Classic Cherry-Picking**  
   The regime quotes only the non-interference clause while ignoring the Charter’s requirements regarding democracy, human rights, and collective responsibility for regional peace.

---

### Conclusion

The Naypyidaw government’s accusation that ASEAN is violating the Charter is **not supported by the full text** of the document. ASEAN is operating within the spirit and balanced framework of the Charter.

By failing to implement the Five-Point Consensus and continuing widespread violence, the regime itself is undermining the very principles it claims to defend.

**“Myanmar-owned and Myanmar-led process”** in practice means:  
“We will continue military rule and violence, and no one is allowed to comment.”

This position is inconsistent with both the letter and the spirit of the ASEAN Charter.
