## Executive Summary

Since 2023, Myanmar’s conflict zones have become a major hub for industrial-scale cyber scam operations. These operations are run by transnational criminal syndicates that exploit:

- Civil war conditions
- Fragmented territorial control
- Porous borders
- Weak rule of law in non-state areas

A significant number of **Chinese nationals** are present in this ecosystem — **as investors, operators, and importantly, as trafficking victims** forced into scam labour.

This briefing analyzes **migration patterns**, **raid observations**, and **trafficking economics** using open reporting, field observations, and documented incidents.

---

## 1. Scale of Chinese Presence in Myanmar (Context)

| Category | Estimated Range | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Long-term ethnic Chinese population | 1.2 – 1.8 million | Mandalay, Yangon, Shan State |
| Scam-compound related presence (2023–2026) | 60,000 – 150,000 | Fluctuating due to raids, repatriations, relocations |

These numbers are derived from:
- UNODC reporting on scam compounds
- Documented mass raids (KK Park, Shwe Kokko, Min Lat Pan / Shunda Park)
- Chinese repatriation announcements (tens of thousands in 2024–2025)
- Field observations during compound crackdowns

---

## 2. Dual Migration Tracks Observed

### Legal / Semi-Legal Track
- Investment in agriculture, mining, casinos, logistics
- Land acquisition in MNDAA, UWSA, TNLA, Karen border zones
- Entry via Thailand on business visas before moving into Myanmar

### Illegal / Criminal Track
- Trafficking via fake job offers across Southeast Asia
- Kidnapping and transport into scam compounds
- Sale into forced labour inside guarded compounds

---

## 3. Observations During Major Raids

During raids such as **Min Lat Pan (Nov 2025)** and earlier operations:

Observers noted:

- Large numbers of Chinese nationals fleeing compounds
- Individuals carrying luggage and cash without escape routes
- Confusion consistent with trafficked labourers rather than criminals
- Testimonies indicating debt bondage and coercion

These scenes strongly indicate **victim trafficking at industrial scale**.

---

## 4. The Forced Labour Market Mechanism

Multiple testimonies and reports indicate a pricing structure:

> Traffickers reportedly receive **~600,000 MMK per delivered Chinese national**

This creates a perverse market dynamic:

> “Bring any Chinese” → payment → delivery into scam compounds

This puts **ordinary Chinese citizens across the region at risk** from criminal recruitment networks.

---

## 5. Strategic Implications

- Conflict zones act as safe havens for organized crime
- Some local armed actors tax or protect compounds
- Criminal networks adapt faster than enforcement actions
- The scam industry generates **tens of billions USD** globally per year

Both **Chinese** and **international** citizens are major victims of the scams themselves.

## Conclusion

Myanmar’s 2026 situation shows a convergence of:

- Civil conflict
- Ungoverned territories
- Transnational organized crime
- Human trafficking of Chinese nationals by Chinese criminal syndicates

This is not a migration story alone.

It is a **criminal logistics system exploiting migration pathways**.
